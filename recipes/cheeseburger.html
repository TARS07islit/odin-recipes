Description

The cheeseburger emerged in the United States in the early 20th century when cooks began adding cheese slices to hamburgers as a simple upgrade. It quickly grew into an American classic, symbolizing diner culture and fast-food innovation. This classic cheeseburger features a juicy beef patty topped with melted cheese, crisp lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles, all sandwiched between a soft bun. Its a simple yet satisfying meal that balances savory flavors with a slight crunch.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices
  • Pickles
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil or butter for cooking
  • Ketchup and mustard (optional)

Steps

  1. Divide ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape into patties.
  2. Season patties with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat a skillet or grill with oil/butter over medium heat.
  4. Cook patties 3 to 4 minutes per side, or until desired doneness.
  5. Place a slice of cheddar cheese on each patty and cover briefly to melt.
  6. Toast the buns lightly on the skillet or grill.
  7. Assemble the burger: bottom bun, lettuce, tomato, patty with cheese, onions, pickles, condiments, top bun.
  8. Serve immediately while hot.
