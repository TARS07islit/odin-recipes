Description

Biryani traces its origins to the Indian subcontinent, evolving from Persian pilaf-style dishes brought by travelers and royal courts. Over centuries, it became a celebrated cultural dish with regional variations across India and beyond. This aromatic Biryani combines basmati rice with spiced meat, yogurt, and fragrant herbs. The dish is layered and cooked slowly, allowing the flavors to blend perfectly, resulting in a rich and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or lamb, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 3 cups water

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan and fry onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and spices; cook until oil separates.
  4. Add the meat and yogurt, cooking until the meat is partially done.
  5. Boil water with salt and whole spices; cook the rice until 70% done.
  6. Layer the rice over the meat in a heavy-bottomed pot.
  7. Sprinkle fresh herbs over the top, cover tightly, and cook on low heat for 20â€“30 minutes (dum cooking).
  8. Serve hot with raita or salad.
